## HAVE TO vs HAVE GOT TO (Cheat Sheet)

You use these to say that **somebody must do something** or **something must happen**.

---

### 1. HAVE TO
- **Meaning:** Outside rules (boss, law, school, or doctor). 
- **Tenses:** You can use it for **Past** (*had to*), **Present**, and **Future** (*will have to*).
- **Questions & Negatives:** Use **do / does / did**.
  - *Do you have to go?* / *I don't have to pay.*

**Examples:**
- I **have to** wear a uniform at work. (Boss's rule)
- She **had to** stay home yesterday. (Past)
- We **will have to** buy new ones. (Future)

---

### 2. HAVE GOT TO
- **Meaning:** Same as "have to", but only for **talking (informal)** and it feels more **urgent** (right now!).
- **Tenses:** **Present ONLY**. (You can't use it for past or future).
- **Questions & Negatives:** Use **have / has** (just switch them with the subject).
  - *Have you got to go?* / *I haven't got to go.*
- **Short form:** In American spoken English, we drop 'have' → just **"got to"** (sounds like **"gotta"**).

**Examples:**
- I **have got to** go now! (Very urgent!)
- She **has got to** see this.
- I **gotta** run! (Informal speaking)

---

### VS (The Main Differences)

| **Feature** | **HAVE TO** | **HAVE GOT TO** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Formal?** | Yes (for writing, essays, work) | No (only for talking with friends) |
| **Tenses** | Past, Present, and Future | **Only Present** |
| **Ask a question** | Do / Does + you/she? | Have / Has + you/she? |
| **How it feels** | Normal "must do" | Stronger, "I need to do it right now!" |

---

**Quick Tip:** For emails/exams → use **have to**. For texting/friends → use **have got to** or **gotta**.
