## TO vs FOR (Cheat Sheet)

Use these to show a reason, a purpose, or who gets something.

---

### The Golden Rule (Most Important!)

- Use **TO** + a **Verb** (Action word: read, go, eat, take).
- Use **FOR** + a **Noun** (Thing or person: help, money, you, me).

---

### More Simple Rules

| **Use "TO" when...** | **Use "FOR" when...** |
| :--- | :--- |
| You talk about an **action** (verbs). <br> *Example: I went **to** buy milk.* | You talk about a **thing** or a **person** (nouns). <br> *Example: I went out **for** milk.* |
| You give something to someone. <br> *Example: I sent an email **to** my boss.* | You do something to help someone. <br> *Example: I bought this **for** you.* |
| You talk about a reason (why you do something). <br> *Example: I study hard **to** pass the test.* | You talk about a benefit (if it is good or bad). <br> *Example: Exercise is good **for** you.* |

---

### VS (Quick Summary)

| **Feature** | **TO** | **FOR** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Comes before** | A **Verb** (action) | A **Noun** (thing/person) |
| **Common use** | Purpose (why): *I called **to** say hi.* | Benefit: *This gift is **for** you.* |

---

**Quick Tip:** When you are not sure, look at the next word!
- If the next word is an **action** (run, eat, help) → use **TO**.
- If the next word is a **thing/person** (money, food, Anna) → use **FOR**.
