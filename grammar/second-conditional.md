## Second Conditional (Cheat Sheet)

Use this to talk about **imaginary**, **unlikely**, or **impossible** situations in the present or future.

---

### Structure

| If-part (condition) | Main part (result) |
| :--- | :--- |
| If + subject + past simple | subject + would + verb |

**Examples:**
- *If I won the lottery, I would buy a big house.*
- *She would travel all over the world if she were rich.*

---

### Two Main Uses

| Use | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Future** | Things that probably won't happen (dreams) | *If I met the Queen, I would say hello.* |
| **Present** | Things that are impossible because they're not true | *If I had his number, I would call him.* (I don't have it) |

---

### Important Rule: "Were" for All Subjects

In the second conditional, use **were** instead of **was** with **I / he / she / it**.

- ✅ *If **I were** you, I wouldn't go out with that man.*
- ✅ *If **it were** mine, I would take care of it.*
- ❌ ~~If I was you~~ (informal, but becoming acceptable)

---

### VS (First vs Second Conditional)

| Feature | First Conditional | Second Conditional |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chance** | Possible / likely | Unlikely / impossible |
| **If-part** | If + **present simple** | If + **past simple** |
| **Example** | *If I have enough money, I'll buy shoes.* | *If I had enough money, I would buy a house.* |

---

**Quick Tip:** The past tense in the *if*-part doesn't mean past time — it shows the situation is **not real**!
